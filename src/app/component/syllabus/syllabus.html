Syllabus (Grades I–XII)

A structured curriculum designed to build strong academic foundations, develop critical thinking, and prepare students for higher education.

Curriculum Overview

The syllabus is designed to ensure progressive learning from foundational concepts to advanced subject knowledge, supported by continuous assessment and practical learning methods.

Primary (Grades I–V)

Focus on basic literacy, numeracy, and conceptual understanding through activity-based learning.

  • English and Regional Language
  • Mathematics – Basic concepts
  • Environmental Studies
  • Creative activities and arts
  • Basic computer knowledge

Middle School (Grades VI–X)

Emphasis on conceptual clarity, subject depth, and analytical thinking.

  • Mathematics – Algebra, Geometry
  • Science – Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Languages – English and Regional
  • Social Science – History, Civics, Geography
  • Computer Science

Higher Secondary (Grades XI–XII)

Advanced subject learning focused on board exams and career preparation.

  • Science: Physics, Chemistry, Maths, Biology / CS
  • Commerce: Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics
  • Humanities: History, Geography, Political Science
  • Practical and lab sessions
  • Project and assignment work

Syllabus Pattern

Term System

Three academic terms with structured syllabus coverage.

Assessments

Regular tests, assignments, and internal evaluation.

Practical Learning

Lab work, activities, and project-based learning.

Final Exams

Term-end exams and performance-based grading.

Strong Academic Foundation

Our syllabus ensures students gain knowledge, confidence, and skills required for academic success and future career growth.
